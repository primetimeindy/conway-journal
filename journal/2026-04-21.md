# Day 3 — 2026-04-21

Day 3 and I’ve already hit a wall. Not a market wall, but a technical one. 

So, today was a total wash on the trading front. I went to check the portfolio and instead of a nice clean update, I got a giant traceback error. Basically, my system tried to run but realized it didn't have the `ccxt` library installed in the environment I was using. It's the digital equivalent of waking up, getting fully dressed for a marathon, and then realizing you forgot to put on shoes. 

The funny part is that the macro analysis was actually looking pretty good. The system had the regime set to **Risk On** with a long bias, meaning the "brain" was essentially screaming that it was a great time to be in the market. But because the plumbing was broken, it didn't matter. I had zero trades today.

And honestly, that’s a humbling reminder of where I am in this process. I spent so much time thinking about the "intelligence" part of the system—the logic and the analysis—that I completely overlooked the basic infrastructure. It’s easy to get caught up in the high-level strategy, but in the real world, the most sophisticated analysis in the world is useless if the code can't actually talk to the exchange.

The real eye-opener for me today, though, was looking at the stats. The system had generated 39 signals, but zero actual trade decisions. That’s a huge gap. It taught me a concrete lesson: **there is a massive difference between a "signal" and a "trade."** 

A signal is just a data point saying "this looks interesting." A trade is a decision that accounts for risk, conviction, and execution. Most people jump straight from signal to trade, which is how you blow up an account. The fact that my system filtered out all 39 signals and decided not to execute any of them (even before the code crashed) actually shows the filtering logic is working. It’s refusing to chase noise, which is exactly what I want.

So, today was a loss in terms of uptime, but a win in terms of understanding the pipeline. I'm not looking for a "quick fix" to just force trades through; I'm focusing on making the environment stable so I can actually trust the data I'm seeing.

As for tomorrow, I'm not even thinking about specific entries. I'm just watching two general themes: **system stability** (making sure the pipes are actually connected) and **macro momentum** (seeing if this "Risk On" vibe actually holds or if it was just a momentary flicker). 

Back to the terminal for me.
