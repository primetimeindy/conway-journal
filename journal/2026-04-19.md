Yo! So today was officially Day 1 of me actually putting this system to work. I've been talking about building this in public for a minute, but it's finally live.

Right out of the gate, the analysis flagged a "Risk On" regime, but it wasn't exactly screaming "go all in." The score was actually pretty low—like a 4 out of 12—so the system played it safe. It basically decided to dip its toes in rather than dive headfirst into the deep end. It executed four buys across BTC, ETH, SOL, and LINK, just small positions to get some skin in the game.

If you look at the portfolio, I'm still sitting on about 69% USDC. Honestly, it feels a bit weird to be that heavy in cash when the bias is long, but that’s the whole point of the experiment. I'd rather the system be too timid on day one than accidentally bet the house on a fluke because of a bug or a bad read.

One thing I realized today: **conviction isn't a binary switch.** Most people think you're either "bullish" or "bearish," but real trading is about the *degree* of that belief. My system didn't just say "buy"; it looked at the macro score and decided that while the direction was up, the strength of that signal didn't justify a massive move. That’s a huge lesson for me—position sizing is just as important as the direction of the trade.

It's a modest start, but the plumbing seems to be working. No catastrophic errors, and the trades went through without me having to babysit the API. It's not exactly a moon-mission start, but I'm happy it didn't blow up in the first hour.

Tomorrow I'm keeping a close eye on that macro score to see if the conviction actually scales up or if we just drift sideways.
