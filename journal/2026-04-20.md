# Day 2 — 2026-04-20

Man, Day 2 and I’m already failing at the basics. 

I woke up thinking I was ready to let the system rip, but instead, I got hit with a massive traceback error. Turns out I completely missed a dependency—specifically the `ccxt` library—meaning my system basically looked at the market and said, "I don't even know how to talk to the exchange." It’s one of those rookie mistakes that feels especially humbling when you're trying to build something "autonomous."

So, naturally, I had zero trades today. 

The funny part is that the analysis was actually working in the background. If you look at the logs, the system was spotting plenty of opportunities—some scalps and a few swings. But because I hadn't flipped the switch to "live" mode (and because the code was literally broken), everything just stayed in paper mode. 

Looking at the macro side of things, the system is currently tagging the regime as **Risk On**, but the conviction is actually pretty low. It's not a "full send" kind of vibe; it's more like a cautious optimism. I think that's why the system was split on its decisions, flagging some longs and some shorts simultaneously. In a high-conviction bull market, you'd usually see a much more one-sided bias.

This leads me to the big lesson from today: **the gap between "the logic works" and "the code executes" is where most trading systems die.** 

You can spend a hundred hours perfecting a strategy, but if your environment isn't configured or your dependencies are missing, your edge is exactly zero. It’s a reminder that trading is as much about the plumbing as it is about the price action. I spent so much time thinking about the "brain" of the system that I forgot to make sure the "hands" were actually attached. 

It's a bit frustrating, but honestly, I'm glad this happened now rather than when I had a massive position open. It's way better to lose a day of data than to lose a chunk of the portfolio because of a bug in the execution layer.

Tomorrow is all about stability. I need to stop playing with the logic for a second and just make sure the infrastructure is bulletproof. I'm going to double-check the environment setups and make sure the transition from paper to live is seamless so I don't have to manually babysit the terminal every five minutes.

**What I'm watching tomorrow:**
I'll be keeping an eye on the macro regime score to see if that "Risk On" sentiment actually gains some real steam or if it's just a fake-out. I'm also watching for any significant shifts in volatility that might force the system to tighten its risk parameters.
