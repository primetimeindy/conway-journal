# Day 5 — 2026-04-23

Yo, so day 5 was... an experience. I woke up thinking the system was going to tear it up since the macro is looking pretty bullish right now (Risk-On), but instead, I got hit with a giant wall of red text.

Basically, the system had a total meltdown before it could even start. I ran into a "ModuleNotFoundError," which is a fancy way of saying the system forgot how to talk to the exchange because a piece of the plumbing was missing. It's honestly kind of hilarious in a painful way—imagine having a world-class chef ready to cook a five-course meal, but then realizing you don't actually have any pots or pans. 

The most frustrating part? The analysis was actually humming along in the background. It flagged 39 different signals. In a normal world, that's a lot of potential opportunity. But because the execution side was broken, it didn't matter. Zero trades. 

It's a humbling reminder that in this game, **the plumbing matters just as much as the strategy**. You can spend a thousand hours refining your entry logic and building the perfect "brain," but if your execution layer is flaky, you're just a spectator. I've spent so much time focusing on the "what" and "when" of trading that I neglected the "how" of the actual infrastructure. Theoretical alpha is great, but it's worth exactly zero if the code can't actually hit the "buy" button.

The win here, if you can call it that, is finding this out now rather than during a massive market swing where a bug could actually cost me money. I'd rather lose a day of trading to a missing library than lose a chunk of my portfolio to a logic error during a flash crash. I'm treating this as a lesson in operational risk. Now I know I need a more robust way to verify that the system is actually "healthy" before I trust it to run solo.

So, today was a total wash on the PnL front, but a big win for the "don't trust your tools blindly" department. I'm spending the evening cleaning up the environment and making sure the connection to the exchange is rock solid.

As for tomorrow, I'm not looking for specific entries yet. I'm mostly watching **overall market liquidity** and **sentiment stability**. I want to see if this "risk-on" mood is actually sustainable or if we're just seeing a temporary spike before a mean reversion kicks in. If the plumbing is fixed, I'll let the system tell me where the value is.
