Yo, Day 6 update. Honestly, today was one of those days that really tests your patience, especially since it's a Friday and the temptation to just "do something" is high.

Now, here is the kicker: the system actually generated 39 signals. Thirty. Nine. But it didn't pull the trigger on a single one of them. 

At first, I felt like maybe something was broken or the filters were too tight. But when I dug into the "why," it actually made a lot of sense. The analysis was seeing a lot of movement, but none of it had the conviction or the risk-reward profile required to actually put capital at risk. It was basically the system saying, "Yeah, things are moving, but it's all noise. Don't touch it."

And honestly? That's a huge win. 

The biggest lesson I'm taking away from today is the difference between a **signal** and a **setup**. A signal is just a data point—it's a "hey, look over here" from the math. A setup is when that signal aligns with the macro environment, risk parameters, and conviction levels to create a high-probability entry. Most traders lose money because they treat every signal like a setup. Building this system is teaching me to be okay with the boredom of doing nothing when the setup isn't actually there.

Portfolio is sitting around $1,280 right now. No gains today, but more importantly, no stupid losses. I've spent too many years in this space forcing trades just because I wanted to feel productive. Letting the system be the "adult in the room" and tell me to sit on my hands is exactly why I'm building this.

I'm not going to lie, it's a bit frustrating to see "Risk On" on the dashboard and then just watch the clock run out, but that's the game. If you can't handle the days where the system tells you to do nothing, you'll never survive the days where it tells you to go all in.
