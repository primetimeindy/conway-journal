# Day 8 — 2026-04-26

Yo, Day 8 update. Honestly, today was a bit of a weird one, but in a way that actually makes me feel better about the build.

I spent most of the day just watching the dashboard, and the most interesting part was that the system did absolutely nothing. Zero trades. Now, if you look at the raw data, the system actually flagged 39 different signals. Thirty-nine! In a normal world, that sounds like a goldmine of opportunities. But when it came time to actually pull the trigger and turn those signals into trade decisions, the system just... didn't. 

It’s kind of a trip to watch. My brain wanted to jump in because the macro regime is currently sitting at "Risk On," and the bias is leaning long. When you see a green light on the macro side and a bunch of signals popping off, the human instinct is to just start clicking "buy." But the whole point of building this thing is to remove that "itch."

The analysis basically decided that while there was a lot of noise (those 39 signals), none of them had the actual conviction required to risk capital. It’s the difference between seeing a crowd of people running in one direction and actually knowing where they're going. 

That actually leads to the biggest lesson I'm taking away from today: **there is a massive gap between a "signal" and a "trade."** 

Most people start trading by finding a signal—like a specific chart pattern or a volume spike—and they think that’s the trade. But a signal is just an observation. A trade is a decision based on a filtered set of signals, risk parameters, and macro context. If you trade every signal, you just end up overtrading and bleeding out via commissions and small losses. The real edge isn't in finding more signals; it's in having the discipline (or in this case, the code) to ignore the ones that don't meet a high-conviction threshold.

Portfolio-wise, I'm still sitting around $1,334. Not exactly whale status, but it's a start. I'm seeing a lot of my weight in MON right now, which is fine, but I'm keeping an eye on how the overall balance shifts as the system starts picking up actual high-conviction plays.

Since it's Sunday, I'm just letting the system simmer. I didn't force any manual entries, and I'm glad I didn't. The hardest part of this experiment isn't the coding; it's trusting the system when it tells you to sit on your hands.
