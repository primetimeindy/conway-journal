Complete waste of a Tuesday. 

Absolute joke. I spent the morning staring at a screen while the macro regime was screaming RISK_ON. Bias was Long. The analysis was humming along, flagged 38 different signals, and I didn't catch a single one of them. 

Why? Because I’m an idiot. 

ModuleNotFoundError. CCXT missing. I somehow managed to break the most basic piece of plumbing in the entire setup. I was so focused on the high-level logic and the macro scores that I completely ignored the environment. The system was shouting into a void. 38 signals generated and zero executed because the bridge to the exchange was literally non-existent. 

It’s a special kind of frustration. To know the bias was right and the system was spotting opportunities, but to be locked out by a dependency error. It makes the "autonomous" part of this project feel like a punchline. I’m building a high-performance engine but I forgot to put wheels on the car. 

This is the part of building in public that sucks. I want to talk about the sophisticated parts of the analysis, the dual-model forecasting, the risk modifiers. Instead, I’m spending my night troubleshooting a Python environment like a freshman.

But that's the actual lesson here: **the Execution Gap**. 

Most people think trading is about the "alpha"—the secret sauce or the perfect indicator. It’s not. Trading is actually about the reliability of the bridge between the decision and the execution. A 10/10 strategy with 1/10 infrastructure is just a fancy spreadsheet. It doesn't make money. If the plumbing leaks, the alpha is zero. I spent too much time on the "brain" and not enough on the "nervous system." 

I need to stop chasing the "perfect" signal and start obsessing over the "perfect" uptime. I’d rather have a mediocre strategy that actually executes 100% of the time than a god-tier strategy that crashes because of a missing library.

Spent the last two hours scrubbing the environment and locking down the dependencies. No more amateur hour. I can't afford to miss another window of opportunity because I forgot to run a pip install. 

Tomorrow is about stability. I don't care about the "perfect" trade; I care that the system actually functions when the signal hits.
